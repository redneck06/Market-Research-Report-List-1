<p><p>Health care waste management refers to the proper disposal of medical waste generated by healthcare facilities. There are various market types for managing such waste, including incineration, autoclaves, and others. Incineration involves burning the waste at high temperatures, reducing it to ash and reducing the volume. Autoclaves use steam sterilization to deactivate pathogens in the waste, making it safer for disposal. Other methods may include microwave treatment, chemical disinfection, and landfilling. Each method has its advantages and disadvantages, and healthcare facilities choose the most suitable option based on factors such as cost, efficiency, and environmental impact.</p></p>
