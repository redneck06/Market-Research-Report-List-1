<p><p>Lathe centers are key components used in machining operations to secure and rotate the workpiece. There are two main types of lathe centers: rotary type and fixed type. Rotary type centers consist of a rotating headstock center and a revolving or live tailstock center, allowing for the precise and continuous rotation of the workpiece. Fixed type centers, on the other hand, are stationary and do not rotate, but provide stability and support to the workpiece during machining. These two market types cater to different needs and preferences in a variety of machining applications.</p></p>
