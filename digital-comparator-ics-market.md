<p><p>Digital comparator ICs are electronic components used to compare the digital inputs provided to them and generate a specific output depending on the relationship between the inputs. Identity comparators compare the binary inputs to determine if they are exactly equal, outputting a logical high or low accordingly. On the other hand, magnitude comparators compare the magnitude of the binary inputs to determine which one is greater or lesser, providing an appropriate output signal. These ICs are widely used in various applications, such as digital systems, data processing, and control circuits.</p></p>
