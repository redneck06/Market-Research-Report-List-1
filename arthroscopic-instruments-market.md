<p><p>Arthroscopic instruments can be categorized into two main types: handhold and benchtop. Handhold instruments are designed to be held by hand during arthroscopic procedures, providing surgeons with precision and control. These instruments are typically lightweight and easy to maneuver. On the other hand, benchtop instruments are larger and are meant to be placed on a stable surface during procedures. They offer additional stability and support for more complex arthroscopic surgeries. Both types of instruments play a crucial role in ensuring successful arthroscopic procedures.</p></p>
