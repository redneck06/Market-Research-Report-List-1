<p><p>Tactile and dimensional printing refer to the creation of textured and three-dimensional elements on various surfaces. Gravure printing employs engraved cylinders to transfer ink onto materials, producing high-quality images. Flexography uses flexible plates to print on a wide range of substrates such as cardboard and plastic. Screen printing involves pushing ink through a stencil onto the desired surface. Inkjet printing propels tiny droplets of ink onto materials, allowing for precise and detailed designs. These printing technologies cater to diverse market needs, offering options for visually appealing and tactile printed materials.</p></p>
