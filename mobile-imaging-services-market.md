<p><p>Mobile imaging services refer to the provision of various diagnostic imaging technologies at a patient's location, rather than at a fixed healthcare facility. The market encompasses different types of mobile imaging services, including X-ray, CT (computed tomography), ultrasound, MRI (magnetic resonance imaging), nuclear imaging, mammography, and others. These services allow for convenient and efficient medical imaging, enabling early diagnosis and treatment. X-ray provides detailed images of the body's internal structures, while CT offers cross-sectional imaging. Ultrasound uses sound waves to produce images, MRI uses magnetic fields, and nuclear imaging uses radiopharmaceuticals. Mammography focuses on breast imaging. Other mobile imaging services could include bone density scans and PET.</p></p>
