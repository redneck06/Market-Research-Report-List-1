<p><p>The LVDT (Linear Variable Differential Transformer) Linear Position Sensor is a type of position sensor that measures linear displacement. It consists of a primary coil and two secondary coils wound on a hollow cylindrical core. When a ferromagnetic core is inserted into the core, it induces voltage changes in the secondary windings, which are used to determine the linear position of the core.</p><p>The LVDT Linear Position Sensor has gained significant traction in various industries such as aerospace, automotive, healthcare, and industrial machinery, due to its high accuracy, reliability, and durability. <p><p>The LVDT (Linear Variable Differential Transformer) Linear Position Sensor market can be categorized into two types based on their sensing methods: non-contact position sensor and contact position sensor. Non-contact position sensors use technologies such as magnetostrictive, capacitive, or inductive to measure linear displacement without physically touching the object being measured. Contact position sensors, on the other hand, rely on physical contact with the object to determine its linear position. Both types of sensors have their own advantages and applications, depending on the specific requirements of the industry or application.</p></p>
