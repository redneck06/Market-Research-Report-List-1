<p><p>Contrast media, also known as contrast agents, are substances used to enhance the visibility of internal body structures during medical imaging procedures. There are three main types of contrast media: Iodinated, Gadolinium-Based, and Barium-Based. Iodinated contrast media contain iodine and are most commonly used in X-rays and computed tomography (CT) scans. Gadolinium-based contrast media contain gadolinium and are used in magnetic resonance imaging (MRI). Barium-based contrast media contain barium sulfate and are used in gastrointestinal studies. Each type of contrast media has specific applications and properties, enabling healthcare professionals to obtain clearer and more detailed images for accurate diagnoses.</p></p>
