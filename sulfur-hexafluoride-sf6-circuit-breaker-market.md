Porcelain Pole Structure refers to a circuit breaker that uses porcelain poles to support the interrupter unit and other components. This type is typically used for lower voltage applications. On the other hand, Tank-type Structure refers to a circuit breaker that consists of a sealed tank filled with SF6 gas, which is used as an arc-extinguishing medium. This type is commonly used for higher voltage applications.</p></p>
