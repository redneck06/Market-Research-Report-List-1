<p><p>Natural gas utilities are commonly categorized into two market types: free type and dissolved type. The free type market refers to a system where natural gas is freely accessible to customers, who are responsible for their own distribution systems. In contrast, the dissolved type market involves an integrated utility that manages the distribution of natural gas through its own infrastructure. The main distinction lies in the ownership and control of the distribution network, with the free type market being decentralized and the dissolved type market being centralized.</p></p>
