<p><p>The smart camera market is predominantly divided into two types: CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) and CCD (Charge-Coupled Device). CMOS cameras use an image sensor with an integrated circuit to convert light into digital signals, offering lower power consumption and enhanced integration capabilities. On the other hand, CCD cameras use a more traditional technology that converts light into electrical charges, providing higher image quality and sensitivity. Both types have their own advantages and are widely used in various applications, including surveillance, automotive, industrial, and consumer electronics sectors.</p></p>
