<p><p>In Vitro Diagnostics Quality Control (IVD QC) is a critical process that ensures the accuracy and reliability of diagnostic test results obtained from in vitro diagnostic devices. The market is primarily driven by factors such as the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, rising demand for point-of-care testing, technological advancements, and strict regulatory guidelines for the quality control of diagnostic tests.</p><p>The market is also witnessing various trends that are shaping its growth. One such trend is the adoption of third-party quality control materials. These materials are manufactured by specialized companies and provide an external quality assessment of the diagnostic tests, ensuring their accuracy and reliability. This trend is gaining popularity due to the increased focus on quality assurance and improving the overall performance of diagnostic tests.</p><p>Another trend observed in the market is the introduction of innovative quality control solutions.
