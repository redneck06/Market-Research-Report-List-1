<p><p>Laser cooling systems are used to slow down and control the motion of atoms or molecules. There are several types of laser cooling systems. Doppler cooling works by using the Doppler effect to cool atoms. Sisyphus cooling uses a combination of lasers to create a "trapping potential" that slows down atoms. Resolved sideband cooling involves using lasers to excite the atomic energy levels and then cooling the atoms as they emit photons. Raman sideband cooling relies on the Raman effect to cool atoms. There are also other types of laser cooling systems that employ different techniques to achieve cooling.</p></p>
