<p><ul><li>Chemical Debonding</li><li>Hot Sliding Debonding</li><li>Mechanical Debonding</li><li>Laser Debonding</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Thin wafers temporary bonding equipment and materials refer to the technologies used for bonding and subsequent debonding of thin wafers during semiconductor and electronics manufacturing processes. The market types include chemical debonding, which involves using chemical agents to dissolve the bonding material; hot sliding debonding, where heat and pressure are applied to separate the wafer; mechanical debonding, which uses mechanical force or adhesive materials for detachment; and laser debonding, a non-contact method that employs laser beams to remove the bonding layer. These methods facilitate the handling and processing of fragile wafers for various applications.</p></p>
