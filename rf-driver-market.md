<p><p>The RF driver market consists of two main types: active type and passive type. The active type refers to drivers that require a power source and generate a high-power output signal for RF applications. These drivers are suitable for large-scale communication systems such as cellular networks. On the other hand, the passive type drivers do not require a power source and primarily function as impedance transformers to match the impedance between the RF signal source and load. They are commonly used in low-power applications like wireless connectivity devices.</p></p>
