<p><p>Clinical chemistry analyzer reagents are essential for conducting diagnostic tests in clinical laboratories. The market for these reagents can be categorized into various types, including calibrators, controls, standards, and other reagents. Calibrators are used to calibrate the analyzer and ensure accurate measurements. Controls are used to monitor the performance of the analyzer and maintain quality control. Standards serve as reference materials for comparing test results. Other reagents include substrates, enzymes, buffers, and detergents that are necessary for various biochemical reactions in the analyzer.</p></p>
