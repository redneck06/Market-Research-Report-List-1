<p><p>Inspection cameras are categorized into three types based on the spectrum they operate in. Ultraviolet spectrum cameras use ultraviolet light to detect flaws in objects that are not easily visible to the human eye. Infrared spectrum cameras capture images by detecting heat radiation emitted by objects, allowing for temperature analysis and uncovering hidden defects. Visible spectrum cameras, the most common type, rely on visible light to capture images and are widely used for general inspection purposes. These different camera types cater to specific applications and provide valuable insights in different industries.</p></p>
