<p><p>Positive Material Identification (PMI) is a process used to verify the composition and quality of materials, particularly metals, in various industries. The PMI market primarily consists of two techniques: X-Ray Fluorescence (XRF) and Optical Emission Spectrometry (OES). XRF employs X-rays to analyze the elemental composition of a material, while OES uses optical emissions generated by the material under high temperatures. Both techniques enable accurate identification, reducing the risk of material mix-up and ensuring product integrity and safety in industries such as manufacturing, oil and gas, aerospace, and construction.</p></p>
