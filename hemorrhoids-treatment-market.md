<p><p>Hemorrhoids, also known as piles, refer to the inflammation and swelling of veins in the lower rectum and anus area. Hemorrhoids can occur both internally and externally, causing discomfort, pain, itching, and bleeding. In most cases, hemorrhoids can be treated without surgery through various treatment methods.</p><p>The treatment options for hemorrhoids include lifestyle changes, over-the-counter medications, non-surgical procedures, and surgical interventions. Lifestyle changes involve adopting a high-fiber diet, drinking plenty of fluids, and regular exercise to soften stools, reduce straining, and promote regular bowel movements. Over-the-counter medications like creams, ointments, and suppositories can help provide temporary relief from symptoms. Non-surgical procedures such as rubber band ligation, sclerotherapy, and infrared coagulation are used to shrink hemorrhoids. In severe cases, surgical interventions like hemorrhoidectomy may be necessary.</p><p>The global hemorrhoids treatment market is experiencing significant growth, driven by factors such as the increasing prevalence of hemorrhoids, sedentary lifestyles, dietary changes leading to constipation, and the rising aging population.
