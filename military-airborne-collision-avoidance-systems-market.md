<p><p>Military Airborne Collision Avoidance Systems (ACAS) are designed to prevent in-flight collisions, which can occur due to crowded airspace or poor visibility conditions. Radar-based systems use radio waves to detect and track nearby aircraft, providing pilots with situational awareness. Traffic Alert and Collision Avoidance Systems (TCAS) actively interrogate the transponders of nearby aircraft to generate collision alerts. Portable Collision Avoidance Systems (PCAS) provide additional protection for military aircraft during critical phases of flight. FLARM and GPWS systems give pilots audio and visual warnings of potential collisions. Synthetic Vision, TAWS, and OCAS provide advanced visual displays and terrain information to enhance situational awareness.</p></p>
