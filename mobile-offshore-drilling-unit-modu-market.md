<p><p>A Mobile Offshore Drilling Unit (MODU) refers to various types of drilling platforms used in offshore oil and gas exploration. The three main market types of MODUs are Jackup, Semisubmersible, and Drillship. Jackup units are non-floating structures, equipped with retractable legs that can be jacked up above the water surface. Semisubmersibles are floating units that partially submerge themselves by ballasting their hulls. Drillships are fully floating vessels with drilling capabilities. Each type caters to different water depths and environmental conditions, allowing for efficient offshore drilling operations.</p></p>
