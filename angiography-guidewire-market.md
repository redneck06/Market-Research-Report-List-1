<p><p>Angiography guidewire is a medical device that is used to navigate the blood vessels during angiography procedures. It is a thin and flexible wire that is inserted into the blood vessels, guiding the catheter to the desired site for diagnosis or treatment. Angiography guidewires are commonly used in the diagnosis and treatment of cardiovascular diseases, including coronary artery disease, peripheral artery disease, and vascular diseases.</p><p>The angiography guidewire market is expected to witness significant growth in the forecast period. The growing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, increasing demand for minimally invasive procedures, and technological advancements in guidewire materials and design are the primary factors propelling market growth. Additionally, the rise in the geriatric population and the increasing adoption of interventional procedures are also contributing to market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is witnessing various trends that are shaping its growth. One such trend is the development of hydrophilic guidewires, which offer better lubricity and facilitate smooth navigation through tortuous blood vessels. Another trend is the introduction of nitinol-based guidewires, known for their superior shape memory and flexibility. Moreover, the adoption of disposable and single-use guidewires is also increasing due to concerns regarding infection control and cost-effectiveness.</p><p>Overall, the angiography guidewire market is expected to experience substantial growth, driven by factors such as the high prevalence of cardiovascular diseases and technological advancements.
