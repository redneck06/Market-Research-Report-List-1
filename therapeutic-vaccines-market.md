<p><p>Therapeutic vaccines aim to treat diseases by stimulating the immune system to target specific disease antigens. Peptide therapeutic vaccines utilize small protein fragments to trigger an immune response, whereas cell therapeutic vaccines use immune cells that have been manipulated in the lab to express disease antigens. Nucleic acid therapeutic vaccines use genetic material, such as DNA or RNA, to encode disease antigens and elicit an immune response. All three types have the common goal of harnessing the immune system's power to fight diseases and potentially provide effective treatment options.</p></p>
