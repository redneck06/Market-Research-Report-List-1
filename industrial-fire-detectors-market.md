Heat detector types are designed to detect changes in temperature, triggering an alarm when a certain threshold is exceeded. Ionization detector types detect smoke particles through an ionization process, while photoelectric detector types use a beam of light to detect smoke. Ionization/photoelectric detectors combine both approaches for enhanced sensitivity. Additionally, there are other types of detectors for specific applications, such as flame detectors and gas detectors.</p></p>
