<p><p>Automated irrigation systems are used to efficiently water agricultural fields, gardens, and landscapes. Timer-based irrigation systems utilize pre-set timers to control the watering schedule, ensuring plants receive water at specific times. Drip irrigation involves the slow release of water directly to plant roots, reducing evaporation and water wastage. Sprinkle irrigation uses sprinklers to distribute water over a wider area, mimicking natural rainfall. Other types of automated irrigation systems may include smart systems that use sensors to monitor soil moisture levels and adjust watering accordingly, or subsurface irrigation that delivers water directly to the root zone.</p></p>
